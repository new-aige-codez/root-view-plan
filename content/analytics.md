# Analytics & Projections

*All market-size figures are estimates unless labeled "measured." Where sources conflict, ranges are shown. Treat TAM/SAM/SOM math as order-of-magnitude and assumption-driven.*

---

## The Core Problem (Measured)

AROYA's own cultivation guide tells growers to record runoff by hand:

> *"It is good practice to check and record the Volume, PH and EC of the leachate. This can be recorded in AROYA using Manual Readings."*

Even on the most advanced precision-agriculture cannabis platform, per-zone runoff chemistry remains a **manual bucket-and-meter task**. AROYA's automated Drip and Drain Station covers volume and EC only on a combined (non-isolated) drain stream; pH sensor launched Spring 2026; **no per-zone isolation** on any competitor device.

---

## Market Context: Contracting but Professionalizing

US cannabis cultivation license count: **17,013** (Emerald Intel, Dec 2025 — *measured*), down **8.6% year-over-year**. The market is contracting, not growing.

This is a bullish signal for precision tools. License consolidation removes casual operators; survivors are larger, more sophisticated facilities with higher willingness-to-pay for crop-steering data. A shrinking operator count at rising average sophistication is the exact environment that rewards a $2,500 precision instrument over a $25 bucket.

## Labor Cost Displacement

Manual runoff monitoring (bucket + meter + manual data entry, ~3–4 checks/day across zones) costs an estimated **$4,000–$12,000/yr per facility** in labor time (midpoint ~$7,400/yr). At a device price of $500–$2,500, grower payback is **1–3 years** — before counting data quality improvements from per-zone isolation vs. blended grab samples.

This frames the product as cost-justified to the buyer, not just a nice-to-have.

## Competitive Landscape

| Competitor | What It Does | Gap vs. Root View |
|---|---|---|
| **AROYA Drip and Drain** (Addium/METER) | Automated EC + volume + temperature on combined drain stream | No per-zone isolation; pH launching Spring 2026; volume not isolated per zone |
| **Ridder DrainChecker** (Netherlands) | Per-zone drain measurement for greenhouse substrate gutters | Dutch commercial greenhouse focus; no cannabis software; sequential valve isolation architecture differs; no AROYA compatibility |
| **Runoff Copilot** (Soil Moisture Sense, UK) | Volume monitoring for entire bag runs; optional EC/pH; solar, cellular | Aggregate run volume only; no per-zone sequential isolation; horticulture focus |
| **TrolMaster Aqua-X** | Substrate WC + irrigation control (up to ~30 zones) | No automated runoff-chemistry isolation |
| **Growlink** | TDR substrate WC/EC + IoT controllers | No runoff chemistry isolation |
| **Bluelab Combo Meter** ($339) | Industry-standard manual pH + EC meter | Manual only — exactly the workflow Root View automates |
| **Pulse / Trym / Argus / Priva** | Environmental/substrate monitoring | None perform automated per-zone runoff EC/pH sampling |

**Root View's differentiation:** sequential per-zone isolation via normally-closed motorized ball valves — one zone's runoff sampled at a time, samples never mix — yielding clean EC + pH + volume per zone. Ridder DrainChecker narrows (but does not eliminate) the "no competitor does per-zone" claim; Root View's moat is the **valve isolation method patent** + cannabis-software integration.
