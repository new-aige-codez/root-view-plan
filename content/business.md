# Business & Exit

## 1-Page Business Plan

**Root View** is a patented, automated hydroponic runoff measurement device that provides per-zone EC, pH, and volume data to precision-agriculture cannabis and CEA produce growers — replacing a manual bucket-and-meter workflow that even the market leader (AROYA) currently requires.

**The patent** covers the sequential per-zone isolation method: normally-closed motorized ball valves at each drain line open one at a time, so samples never mix. Filed as a PCT (May 26, 2026), with provisional filed May 28, 2025. Four embodiments cover trough-integrated and plumbing-inline configurations.

**The prototype** (V.2, in build) uses a Waveshare ESP32 2.8" touchscreen, DF Robot pH sensors, ultrasonic flow meter, and US Solid 1" ball valves. Designed for retrofit into standard ~4"W × 5"D floor-drain troughs. Modular: one master unit + add-on remote valves per table.

**The market** is ~17,000 US cannabis cultivation sites (contracting but professionalizing) + ~3,000 US CEA produce operations. Standalone revenue opportunity is low single-digit millions; the primary thesis is strategic/defensive value to an established platform.

**The preferred exit** is a partial acquisition + royalty licensing deal, prioritizing AROYA (Addium) as the primary target given their documented manual-runoff-entry gap and warm contact through Rob's beta-tester relationship.

**The team:** Brian Brubaker (R&D, engineering, IP — 40%) and Rob Tabatabaei (operations, cannabis facility expertise, market access — 60%). Rob manages JAE LLC, a licensed cannabis cultivation facility in Downtown Los Angeles.

## Detailed Business Plan

### Problem
Runoff monitoring in drain-to-waste hydroponics is the precision-agriculture industry's most notable remaining manual workflow. AROYA's platform — the market standard — explicitly instructs growers to "check and record the Volume, PH and EC of the leachate" by hand. Per-zone chemistry data is critical for crop steering: runoff EC vs. feed EC delta reveals nutrient stacking; runoff pH reveals lockout risk; runoff % guides irrigation scheduling. Facility-average data from a combined drain stream masks zone-level divergence — the exact data problem serious cultivators need solved.

### Solution
Root View automates this workflow through a patented sequential-isolation architecture: drain-line valves open one zone at a time, the sensor chamber captures a clean isolated sample, measurements are taken, the chamber is flushed, and the device moves to the next zone. No human intervention required. Data syncs to a companion app/dashboard. The sensor chamber's auto-flush and calibration-solution top-off extend intervals between manual calibration.

### Target Customer
Mid-to-large cannabis cultivation facilities (100+ lights / multiple zones) that:
- Already use crop-steering practices (EC/pH management)
- Track runoff manually today (bucket + Bluelab meter)
- Use a platform like AROYA that accepts manual runoff readings

Secondary market: rockwool/coco drain-to-waste produce growers (Dutch-model greenhouse tomatoes, peppers, strawberries) — analogous workflow, analogous need.

### Revenue Model
Hardware sale (multi-zone install) + optional subscription for cloud data/app access. Rough pricing benchmark: $3,000 for a 3-zone master unit; additional remote valve modules ~$X each (TBD). Subscription modeled on AROYA's SaaS structure.

Under a licensing deal, revenue comes from: upfront license fee + per-unit royalty (target 5–8% of net sales) + annual minimums. This is the preferred structure — Brian and Rob retain IP ownership while a platform partner handles sales/distribution.

## Competitive Landscape Summary

The market has no automated per-zone isolated runoff device. Competitors either:
- Measure a blended combined drain stream (AROYA Drip and Drain — no isolation, no pH until 2026)
- Measure aggregate run volume without per-zone isolation (Runoff Copilot)
- Monitor substrate moisture/EC but not drain chemistry (TrolMaster, Growlink)
- Are manual reference instruments Root View replaces (Bluelab)

The gap is documented, the pain is real, and AROYA has acknowledged it implicitly by announcing a pH sensor for Drip and Drain — while the per-zone isolation problem remains unsolved.
